Industry-standard options pricing relies on the Black-Scholes formula, $$C = N(d_1)S_t-N(d_2)K e^{-r(T-t)}$$ $$\rm{for}\ d_1=\frac{\ln{S_t/K}+\left(r+\sigma^2/2\right)(T-t)}{\sigma\sqrt{(T-t)}}$$ $$\rm{and}\ d_2=d_1-\sigma\sqrt{T-t}$$ the solution to the Black-Scholes equation for a European option under the assumption of constant implied volatility and a constant risk-free rate. Another approach to options pricing utilizes binomial trees, but these models traditionally assume constant volatility and risk-free interest rate as well. This app prices options with a binomial tree and variable implied volatility. These variable volatilities are predicted by a model trained on historical variability data and Black-Scholes input variables (underlying stock price, risk-free interest rate, market price of the option, strike price, and time to expiration). More advanced models are under development, trained on news sentiment analysis and past implied volatility data.
